OVERVIEW

 

Experience:

I am a Lead Interaction Designer, Service Architect and Digital Product Strategist.

My work includes a broad mix of web-based SAAS and mobile applications for Fortune 1000 companies as well as concept-stage start-ups.

I've had the good fortune to work on a particulary broad set of vertical concerns including Finance, Consumer Banking, Travel, Hospitality, Pharma, Beverage, Retail, Education and Government. Having a view into so many industries has allowed me to see the commonalities of all sort of companies which is pretty valuable when trying to locate the essential aspect of a new project.

I really enjoy designing software that has multiple user audiences with distinct needs. I know I'm in the right place if the project requires the creation and adoption of accompanying business processes.

Additionally, I have a unique perspective on the relationship between advertising agencies and media having worked as both a service provider and client of major digital agencies on custom advertising products.

I think I'm happiest working at a product strategy level these days or at least like to be critically involved in defining the problem space for product requirements. I find I'm most interested in the reasons why particular service and set of features works for a given user base. As a result, I'm always thrilled when there's an opportunity to do proper end-user research for a software product's roadmap.

Telehealth System

Product Strategy, Functional Analysis, Interaction Design

Overview:

For this homecare nursing application, I had a hybrid role where I contributed product architecture and functional analysis tasks to a telehealth system and served as a final set of eyes for the interaction and visual designers. This project was interesting to me because there were in effect two very distinct user roles with totally different product experiences. On the one hand, we interviewed homecare nurses about their reporting needs, daily taskflow and device usage patterns. On the other hand, the patients had a vastly different user experience where an Android TV and tablet presented them with health questions and recorded biometric information from bluetooth devices like a blood pressure cuff and weight scale. I'm most proud of coming up with some very flexible methods that allowed for custom risk weighting to be normalized and plugged into the visualizations for each client nursing organization.
